Special relativity says time dilation is given by
\begin{equation}
  \Delta t_{fixed}=\gamma \Delta t_{moving}
  \label{eqn:time-dilation}
\end{equation}
where $$\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v_{moving}^2/c^2}}.$$
Assuming the timeline in the movie is correct and they're undergoing a $\qty{30}{\year}$ round trip, with say $\qty{4}{\year}$ to do stuff at Tau Ceti,
we get the expected $\Delta t_{fixed}\approx \qty{13}{\year}$ trip time. This is the time for an Earth observer. Assuming no encounters with
massive objects, we can rearrange \cref{eqn:time-dilation} to find $v_{moving}$,
\begin{align*}
  v_{moving}&=c\sqrt{1-(\Delta t_{fixed})^{-2}(\Delta t_{moving})^{2}}\\
  &= \qty{2.852483008 e8}{\meter\per\second}\\
  &\approx 0.95c.
  \label{eqn:velocity-time-dilation}
\end{align*}
Definitely a reachable speed. Assuming no gravity assists (which is not the case, when Grace first sees the path he took out of the solar system a gravity assist is evident),
you could accelerate at 1.5 times Earth's gravity for just over half a year and reach this speed.
